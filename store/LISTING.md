## Name (≤ 45 chars)

ClipVault — Clip images & text to a library

## Summary / short description (≤ 132 chars)

Right-click to clip images, text, links, and pages into a private, local library. Auto-tag, organize by project, search, export.

## Category

Productivity

## Detailed description

**ClipVault is a low-friction way to collect what you find on the web.**

See an image, a passage, a link, or a whole page worth keeping? Right-click and clip it. ClipVault drops it into a private library that lives entirely on your own device — no account, no cloud, no tracking.

**Capture anything**
- Images (stored as a thumbnail, with the full image saved when possible)
- Text selections
- Links and whole pages
- Videos and tweets are recognized and handled sensibly

**Stay organized without effort**
- Auto-suggested tags when you clip
- Group clips into projects
- Add notes
- Fast search across titles, text, tags, and notes
- A clean waterfall gallery so your images read at a glance

**Fast by design**
- Right-click menu, or a keyboard shortcut (Alt+Shift+C to clip, Alt+Shift+L to open the library)
- A quick save bubble lets you set project, tags, and a note before saving — nothing is stored until you hit Save
- Undo on deletes

**Your data stays yours**
- Everything is stored locally in your browser
- Nothing is ever uploaded — ClipVault has no servers
- Export a full backup as JSON, or a readable Markdown list, anytime
- Import a backup to restore

**Minimal permissions**
ClipVault does not use broad "read and change all your data on all websites" access. It only touches a page at the moment you clip, using the temporary `activeTab` permission.
